## Pre-print release strategy

1. **Format**: combined `preprint.md` includes main manuscript + full SI Appendix (~8,040 words), aimed at standalone reading without the PNAS template constraints.

2. **Timing**: post to SocArXiv 2 weeks before PNAS submission to gather pre-publication peer feedback. Author email is on the title page; community comments help anticipate reviewer concerns.

3. **DOI / archival**: SocArXiv issues a DOI; cite the DOI in the PNAS submission as evidence of public peer-engagement.

4. **Coordinated promotion**: short Twitter/Bluesky thread highlighting:
   - Hindawi natural experiment
   - 0% → 65-95% editorial-tag jump
   - Publisher-mediated reading vs content-mediated narrative
   - Forward-prediction (Spandidos, Taylor & Francis as next-risk)
   - Forest plot of residual mill effect (CS / Social Sciences vs biomedicine split)

5. **Risk mitigation**: pre-print release is openly discussed in the cover letter to PNAS as evidence of methodological transparency and community engagement; PNAS does not bar manuscripts that have appeared on pre-print servers.
