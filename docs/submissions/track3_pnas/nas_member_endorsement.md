# NAS Member Endorsement Strategy — PNAS Direct Submission Plus

## Background

PNAS offers three submission tracks:

| Track | Description | Endorsement | Word limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Direct Submission** | Standard external submission. No NAS member required. | None | ~6 pages |
| **Direct Submission Plus** | Extended-length variant. No NAS member required. | None | ~10 pages |
| **Contributed** | NAS member submits the paper as their own. | NAS member as senior/coauthor | ~6 pages |

**Recommended path: Direct Submission Plus.** The current manuscript fits comfortably in the 9-page PNAS Plus format, and avoids the formal NAS-coauthor requirement of the Contributed track. Endorsement-style outreach to NAS members can still be valuable for reviewer-suggestion legitimacy and post-decision visibility.

## Outreach template

Brief email (200 words) to be customised per recipient:

> Dear Professor [Name],
>
> I am submitting "Citation Palimpsests" to PNAS, and I wanted to make you aware of the work given your published interest in [specific connection].
>
> Using a 479-million-paper citation graph and Hindawi's 2023 mass retraction (9,675 papers in one year, 63% of all paper-mill retractions in the historical record) as a natural experiment, the paper shows that the apparent "paper-mill citation crisis" is overwhelmingly mediated by a single publisher's editorial-process collapse, not by content. Difference-in-differences with synthetic control yields a 2023 treatment effect of +0.47 in zombie-citation rate; placebo on Wiley is null. Within Hindawi, paper-mill and non-mill retractions are statistically indistinguishable (within-Hindawi mill OR = 0.57). Process evidence is striking: peer-review-compromise, AI-content, and third-party-investigation tags within Hindawi rose from 0% in 2017–21 to 65–95% in 2023.
>
> A pre-print is available at [SocArXiv DOI when posted]. The PDF is attached for your reference. I would value any comments before formal submission, and your perspective on the policy implications (publisher-level retraction-lag monitoring as the highest-leverage intervention).
>
> With thanks,
> Suan Lee
> Semyung University

## Tactics

1. **Pre-print first.** Post to SocArXiv before approaching NAS members; give them a stable citation to refer to.
2. **Personalise.** Each email mentions a specific paper or speech of theirs that connects.
3. **Sequential outreach.** Wait 2 weeks between batches to avoid flooding the same network.
4. **No formal endorsement requirement.** Direct Submission Plus does NOT require NAS endorsement; outreach is for awareness, comment, and possible reviewer suggestion.
5. **If endorsement comes, switch to Contributed track.** A Contributed submission with strong NAS endorser sometimes accelerates review (decision in 1–2 months vs 3–6 for Direct Submission). Trade-off: the NAS member becomes co-author and shares responsibility for the work.

## Caveat

NAS-member endorsement is a complement, not a substitute, to manuscript quality. The Direct Submission Plus path is designed for self-standing methodologically rigorous work; the present manuscript meets that bar with the natural-experiment + DID + synthetic control + forward-prediction + process-evidence stack. Pursue endorsement opportunistically rather than as a gatekeeping step.
